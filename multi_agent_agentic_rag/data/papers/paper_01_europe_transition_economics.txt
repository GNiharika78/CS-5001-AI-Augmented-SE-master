Title: Renewable Energy Transition Economics in Europe

Abstract:
This report examines the economic and environmental impacts of renewable energy adoption across major European economies.
Across Germany, France, Spain, and the Netherlands, rising renewable energy shares are associated with lower power-sector
emissions intensity over time. Economic gains are visible through investment growth, job creation in clean-energy sectors,
and improved resilience against fossil-fuel price volatility.

Key Findings:
1. Renewable deployment tends to reduce medium-term exposure to imported fuel shocks.
2. Wind and solar growth often require balancing investments in storage, transmission, and grid modernization.
3. Countries with strong policy continuity tend to attract more private capital into renewable infrastructure.
4. Short-term electricity price pressure can still occur during grid transition periods.
