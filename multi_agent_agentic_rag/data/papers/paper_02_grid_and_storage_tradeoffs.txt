Title: Grid Integration and Storage Trade-Offs in High-Renewable Systems

Abstract:
High shares of variable renewable generation create operational benefits and new planning challenges.
Grid congestion, curtailment, and storage adequacy become central concerns as solar and wind penetration increases.

Key Findings:
1. Grid flexibility investments are essential for sustaining renewable growth.
2. Storage reduces balancing costs but raises capital requirements in the near term.
3. Policy coordination across interconnectors and market design materially affects long-run efficiency.
